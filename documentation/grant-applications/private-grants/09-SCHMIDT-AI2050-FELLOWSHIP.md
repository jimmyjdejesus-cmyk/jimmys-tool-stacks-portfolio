## 2. The Challenge (500 words)

### The Alignment Problem

As AI systems become more capable, the challenge of ensuring they remain beneficial grows more acute. The fundamental problem: we cannot specify all human values in advance, and static systems cannot adapt to changing contexts.

Current approaches to AI alignment face limitations:

**Constitutional AI / RLHF**:
- Relies on human specification of values
- Static after training
- Values encoded may not generalize
- Vulnerable to specification gaming

**Oversight and Control**:
- Assumes humans can evaluate AI behavior
- Breaks down as AI surpasses human capability
- Creates adversarial dynamics
- Doesn't scale with AI deployment

### The Biological Alternative

Biological intelligence offers a different paradigm. Life evolved over 4 billion years, producing organisms that are:

- **Robust**: Handle novel situations gracefully
- **Adaptive**: Continuously learn and change
- **Aligned**: Pursue fitness (survival/reproduction) reliably
- **Cooperative**: Form symbiotic relationships

Crucially, biological alignment wasn't designedâ€”it evolved. Organisms that weren't aligned with their fitness function didn't reproduce. Over time, evolution produced increasingly sophisticated alignment.

### The Opportunity

What if we could apply evolutionary principles to AI development? Not simple genetic algorithms, but the full richness of biological evolution:

- **Phylogenetics**: Lineages that inherit and modify traits
- **Development**: Architectures that grow, not just train
- **Ecology**: Populations that compete and cooperate
- **Symbiosis**: Co-evolution with other species (including humans)
